I am a doctoral student at the University of Calfornia, Davis in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering. I am a member of the BIRD lab where I study the morphing strategies that raptors use for flight control. I am advised by Dr. Christina Harvey and Dr. Kevin P.T. Haughn at the Army Research Laboratory.

I completed my undergraduate degree in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park, conducting research at the Alfred Gessow Rotorcraft Center, learning aerospace control systems, and working at the student run radio station, WMUC 88.1 FM (now, 90.5 FM).

I spent a few years in the Flight Dynamics branch of the Naval Air Systems Command (NAVAIR) where I worked on the F-35 Joint Strike Fighter program, primarily on flight testing of the F-35B (STOVL) variant. I also performed research on rotorcraft pilot workload under augmented reality cueing during shipboard dynamic interface.

I then worked as a software engineer for a number of years, developing tools for the National Reconaissance Office (NRO).

In my spare time I enjoy riding vintage bikes, playing and designing board games, cooking, playing guitar, rock climbing, still photography, and painting. While an amateur, I document my photography on Instagram.
